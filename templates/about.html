Know About Breast Cancer Disease

Breast cancer is a common type of cancer that affects women around the world. It is estimated that one in eight women will develop breast cancer at some point in their lives. Breast cancer occurs when abnormal cells grow uncontrollably in the breast tissue, leading to the formation of a lump or mass.

Early detection of breast cancer is key to successful treatment and recovery. Women should perform regular breast self-exams and undergo mammography screenings as recommended by their healthcare provider. Common signs of breast cancer include a lump or thickening in the breast tissue, changes in the shape or size of the breast, nipple discharge or inversion, and skin changes such as redness or dimpling.

How to Use ealth Center Website

Mammography is an x-ray imaging method used to detect breast cancer and other breast diseases. During a mammogram, the breast is compressed and exposed to x-rays that produce an image, called a mammogram. Low-density tissues appear translucent while dense tissues like tumors appear whiter. Radiologists examine mammograms to identify abnormal regions that could represent cancerous tumors, benign tumors, fibroadenomas, or complex cysts. They look at the size, shape, contrast, and edges of the region, as well as for microcalcifications, which can indicate the presence of cancer.

Understamd the ealth Center Prediction Model

Modern medical image processing techniques use algorithms and machine learning to analyze histopathology images captured by a microscope. Computer-aided mechanisms are applied to obtain better results as compared to manual pathological detection systems, which are tedious and prone to human error. Deep learning is extensively utilized in the medical imaging field, with a focus on binary classification problems to identify whether a tumor is benign or malignant. In deep learning algorithms, tasks involve image preprocessing, segmentation, feature extraction through a convolutional neural network (CNN), and classification using a fully connected network. Machine learning algorithms perform better than a human pathologist and provide more accurate results in medical image processing.
